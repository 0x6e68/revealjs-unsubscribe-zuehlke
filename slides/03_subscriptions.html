Summary

  • Comes "out of the box" with Angular
  • With every ".subscribe(...)" call you get a reference to the subscription
  • A ".unsubscribe(...)" call on the reference will destroy the subscription
  • A "Subscription" instance can hold/handle multiple subscripton references

Summary - (Dis-)advantages

  • (+) Easy to understand / implement
  • (-) Does not let the observable "complete" itself
  • (-) Call on ".unsubscribe()" in OnDestroy can easily be forgotten
  • (-) A very "imperative" solution
